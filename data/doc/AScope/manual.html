AScope Application

The Playback application attempts to emit data recorded by the SideCar system. The application uses the timestamps recorded in the data files to replicate the original arrival rates seen by the SideCar recorder.

Loading Recording Files

You may select a directory containing recorded data in one of two ways: clicking on the Load button and navigating into the directory to load, or typing in the location by hand in the text field to the right of the Load button. The text field also contains a collection of previously-visited directory locations. Simply click on the triangle at the right of the text field to view previous load locations. Selecting one of the shown entries will load the indicated directory.

Channel Activation

Specific data channels may be enabled or disabled by simply clicking on the checkbox to the left of the channel name. Channels without a check mark next to their name will not emit any data when playback is active.

Playback Control

To begin emitting recorded data, simply press the Start button. Likewise to stop, press the button again. The button's label will change depending on whether or not playback is active.

To jump to the beginning of the recorded data, press the Rewind button. You may press this button at any time, even while a playback is active. To jump elsewhere in time, enter a time value in the Jump To field. You can enter relative or absolute time values, like so:

  • +53s — jump 50 seconds ahead
  • -3m — jump 3 minutes back
  • 15.03.23 or 15:03:23 &mdash jump to an absolute time of 15:03:23

Relative times may contain a suffix that describes the time units: h for hours, m for minutes, and s for seconds. If not given the units defauls to seconds. Absolute times must always consist of at least 8 characters, with minutes and seconds always having two digits of value and the hours having two or more.

Both time formats support fractional second values to the right of a decimal point in the time value. For instance, the time value +0.001 will attempt to jump forward by 1/1000th of a second.

Rate Control

To change the rate at which the playback clock runs, click on the Rate widget. Selecting Normal will cause the playback clock to run at the same speed as the wall clock. Selecting values greater than one will cause the playback clock to run faster than real time, while choosing fractional values less than one will cause it to run slower than real time.

Although the rate setting may be changed at any time, for best playback fidelity, stop any active playback before changing the playback rate.

Emitter Control

Unlike the PRI Emitter application, Playback does not support TCP connections, only UDP multicast. You can set the multicast address used by the playback emitters in the Address field.Since each playback emitter is a SideCar data publisher, running more than one Playback application on the local LAN will result in data publisher name conflicts, which the Zeroconf DNS infrastructure will resolve by renaming one of the data publishers. To mitigate this, you can enter your own unique suffix in the Pub Suffix text field. Note that if others choose the same suffix as you, Zeroconf will again have to resolve name conflicts like before.
